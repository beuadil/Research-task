## Post 5

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ramprasannakharvy_have-you-seen-the-latest-seo-study-on-ai-activity-7445451236302262272-cyX9

Have you seen the latest SEO study on AI content?

This research by Kyle Roof tested multiple LLMs for SEO content in 2026. The finding says.

Good writing does not rank. (Kyle has been telling this ever since)

Content aligned with structured, data-driven SEO signals ranks.

I have followed Kyle’s work for a while. His approach stays consistent. He tests assumptions with data, not opinions.

The results support that mindset.
Key findings:
➡️ Average optimization score: 58.41
➡️ Ranking benchmark: 80+
➡️ Models above 80: 1 out of 13

Even strong models fall short:
🢒 GPT-4.1: 72.8
🢒 Gemini 3 Pro: 70.9
🢒 GPT-5.2: 49.1

What did the study measure?
🠖 Title tag presence
🠖 H1 structure
🠖 Subheading range 5 to 9
🠖 Content depth 183 to 305 words
🠖 NLP entity coverage

Where things break:
⤍ Only 2 out of 13 models hit subheading targets
⤍ Only 1 hit content depth targets
⤍ Many skipped basic metadata like title tags

Adding “SEO-optimized” to prompts improved output, but it did not fix structure. This is the gap most teams miss.

AI produces readable content but Search engines rank measurable signals.

So what should you change?

✔ Use AI for ideation and drafts
✔ Apply structured SEO systems for final output
✔ Validate content against ranking signals

If your content looks good but does not rank, you face a structural problem.
