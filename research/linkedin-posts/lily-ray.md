## Post 3

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lily-ray-44755615_can-ai-responses-be-influenced-the-seo-industry-activity-7447048390464503808-JuoB

Remember what happened last time The Verge did a big piece covering the spammy side of the SEO industry? 

“The People That Ruined The Internet”? 🙄

I certainly do, because I was one of the people they interviewed for that article. And right before the article went live - Google dropped the Helpful Content Update. The Verge was one of many publishers at the time calling out spammy SEO results on Google. 

And now with today’s new Verge article calling out spammy GEO tactics - including a response from Google - I’m getting flashbacks to late 2023. 

When the spam and manipulation starts to get a lot of public attention, Google usually (eventually) responds with major countermeasures. And if those countermeasures are super aggressive, they can end up hurting a lot of “innocent” sites that get caught in the collateral damage. 

This is why I tend to be cautious - that collateral damage can be VERY hard to recover from. 

I’ve been monitoring the Google core update that’s currently rolling out and have indeed seen various examples of sites crashing, especially those that started to see declines beginning earlier this year. A lot of scaled content abuse demotions are happening for sites that went too hard on AI-generated content.

But I am still not seeing a ton of evidence that Google is specifically targeting a lot of the new GEO approaches… at least not yet.

Stay tuned, I’ll be providing more updates on this in the coming weeks. 

h/t Glenn Gabe for sharing the article and great quotes from Britney Muller and Rand Fishkin!

Making ads work in AI is incredibly challenging for them. Which is why they only pushed AI in search after ChatGPT launched.

Ads work largely because the vast majority of people don't know they are ads.

And AI by default should give you the best results possible and reduce CTR as people can see an abbreviation of what the business is all about before they click.

That's tough to build an ad model around that doesn't produce less money.
