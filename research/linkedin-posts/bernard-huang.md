## Post 1
> Direct post by Bernard Huang

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/bernardjhuang_the-biggest-shift-in-seo-isnt-technical-activity-7396614567365263360-oUw4

The biggest shift in SEO isn't technical. It's cognitive.

We're no longer typing queries. We're having conversations. That subtle difference changes everything.

For 20 years, SEO revolved around optimizing for what people searched for. Now, AI search is optimizing for how people think.

We're entering a new era defined not by the "long tail," but by what I call the Long Long Tail. It's not about ranking for one keyword — it's about being referenced across thousands of semantically connected ideas.

- One-off content won't cut it.
- Patterns of meaning will.
- Mentions are the new rankings.

## Post 4
> Direct post by Bernard Huang

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/bernardjhuang_everyone-keeps-saying-ai-is-the-new-search-activity-7435061789035253761-lv81

Everyone keeps saying "AI is the new search." But here's the part people skip: if AI is search, then every model is a different search engine and they all behave very differently.

- Gemini feels like Google: constantly searching the web, recalculating, expanding queries.
- GPT feels more like early Yahoo: structured, curated, harder to disrupt quickly.
- Perplexity is basically Bing with excellent citations.
- Grok behaves more like Twitter search than a traditional index.
- Claude feels like Ask Jeeves if Jeeves went to grad school.

When people say "optimize for AI," they're usually treating five completely different retrieval systems like one platform. Visibility works differently in each of them.
