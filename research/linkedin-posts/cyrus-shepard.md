## Post 1
> Referenced content — Mike Morgan summarising Cyrus Shepard's AI SEO playbook

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/whippetdigital_seo-geo-aisearch-activity-7401368032830529536-V3Zl

Cyrus Shepard (16+ years in SEO, formerly Head of SEO at Moz) shared that AI optimisation now makes up 30% of his consulting work. In January, it was zero. By December, he expects 70-80%.

His playbook:

1. Audit content by type — E-commerce and service pages are holding strong; generic informational blogs are getting crushed. If AI can write it without proprietary knowledge, it's at risk.

2. Add proprietary elements AI can't replicate — webinars, interviews, custom data, proprietary info, firsthand experience. One attorney added "expert in X, recognised by Y" to their homepage and AIO citations improved almost immediately.

3. Optimise your homepage for AI parsing — most homepages are under-optimised. Add clear expertise, credentials, and services so AI can extract signals quickly.

4. Track AI visibility, not just rankings — Cyrus runs weekly visibility reports using tools like Gumshoe AI to measure frequency of appearance, sentiment, and competitors cited instead.

5. Accept the automation paradox — social media says "automate everything." Google devalues automated content. What's rising: work with human judgement, curation and real signals. AI assists, it doesn't replace.

6. Prioritise images and detailed how-tos — generic summaries are fading. Step-by-step guides with visuals still perform and survive AI summarisation.

7. Don't chase AI Overview appearances blindly — Cyrus calls AIO visibility the most overhyped trend of 2025. CTR is tiny. Value = brand visibility, not traffic.

5. Accept the automation paradox — the content rising right now and earning links is the content that is NOT automated. Use AI to assist, not replace.

6. Prepare for Google's quality rater changes — Google told raters to identify AI generated content. If your content reads like AI with no human touch, you are at risk.

7. Focus on images and detailed how-tos — visual explanation survives AI summarization better.

8. Do not chase AI Overview appearances blindly — almost no click-through rate. Value is mainly brand awareness, not traffic.

## Post 4
> Referenced content — Chee Chong Lee summarising a Cyrus Shepard x Moz interview on SEO in 2025

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cheechong-lee_seo-in-2025-whats-really-changed-cyrus-activity-7342447253086752768-FWmP

Moz revisited the most popular SEO tactics from the last four years with Cyrus Shepard. Key takeaways:

1. Content quality is non-negotiable — thin, keyword-stuffed pages are out. Value-driven, user-focused content is in.

2. Authority matters more than ever — building trust and credibility is the difference between ranking on page one and being invisible.

3. Technical SEO is evolving — site speed, mobile optimization, and structured data are now table stakes.

4. Outdated link-building tactics are risky — focus on earning links through genuine relationships and high-value content.

5. AI and automation are changing the game — the best marketers are leveraging AI for research, content creation, and analysis.

The fundamentals haven't changed — just the way we execute them. Adaptability is the new superpower.
