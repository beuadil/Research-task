## Post 1
> Direct post by Ryan Law

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_ai-content-wasnt-good-enough-now-it-is-activity-7439350229327302656-3JNC

Until recently, AI content wasn't good enough for SEO. Now, it is. The sooner we can admit that, the more time we have to focus on the parts of marketing where humans will have a longer, happier tenure.

We have reached the point where generative AI can create content indistinguishable from the vast corpus of human-written content produced by content marketers in previous years.

AI has become a more thorough researcher, a more compliant adherent to brand and voice guidelines, more flexible in its response to feedback, faster, and more efficient. There is no longer a trade-off inherent in using AI for content creation.

The path forward will only be found if we are honest about where AI can, and should, be used.

## Post 4
> Direct post by Ryan Law

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_im-just-gonna-say-it-llmo-geo-leo-whatever-activity-7305908021312782338-uHit

LLMO, GEO, LEO — whatever acronym you like — it's just SEO.

Can anyone show me a company who has great visibility in LLMs but not in traditional search? Or vice versa?

The mechanisms through which you can improve LLM visibility:

- Increase your visibility in training datasets and improve brand association with important topics → create content on relevant topics → just SEO
- Improve your visibility in whatever data sources LLMs use for RAG (Bing's and Google's indexes) → just SEO
- Use adversarial examples to trick models into recommending you when they otherwise wouldn't → basically black hat SEO

As more LLMs use traditional search indexes, the boundaries between SEO and LLMO may become even smaller or nonexistent.
