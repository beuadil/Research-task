# We Tested 13 LLMs for SEO Content in 2026 — Here's Why POP AI Still Wins

**Author:** Kyle Roof
**Source:** PageOptimizer Pro Blog

**Date:** March 12, 2026
**Type:** Research study

---

## Overview

PageOptimizer Pro's comprehensive evaluation of 13 large language models for SEO content creation. Core finding: most mainstream LLMs fail to produce structurally optimized content despite strong writing quality.

## Key Finding

Only one model achieved a score exceeding 80 on the POP ranking scale. The highest-scoring alternative scored 72.8. The average non-POP model scored 58.41.

## Testing Methodology

Researchers tested 12 major LLMs plus POP AI Writer using two scenarios:
- **Baseline:** Generic content request without SEO instructions
- **SEO-optimized:** Same request with "SEO-optimized" instruction added

Models tested: Claude Sonnet/Opus, GPT-4.1/5.2, Gemini 3 Pro, DeepSeek V3.x, Llama 4, Mistral Large 3, Grok 4, Perplexity Pro, Qwen 3.5, Doubao 2.0

## Measurement Criteria

- Title tag presence
- H1 page title inclusion
- Subheading count (target: 5–9)
- Main content length (target: 183–305 words)
- Google NLP term coverage (target: 19–86 terms)

## Results

| Model | Score |
|-------|-------|
| POP AI Writer | 100 |
| GPT-4.1 | 72.8 |
| Gemini 3 Pro | 70.9 |
| Average (all others) | 58.41 |
| GPT-5.2 | 49.1 |

Critical gaps: only 2 of 13 models achieved the subheading target. Only POP AI met the content length requirement.

## Key Insight

Adding "SEO-optimized" to prompts improved output slightly but did not fix structural issues. AI produces readable content — search engines rank measurable signals.

## Recommendation

Use conventional LLMs for brainstorming and revision. Use purpose-built SEO AI tools for publication-ready content targeting competitive rankings.
